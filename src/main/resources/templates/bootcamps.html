Lean In IIITSKLM is an initiative motivated by the personal experiences of our core team of female leaders. We hope to establish a community that brings forth opportunities, not only for women but for the entire IIIT SKLM family. A community that ... “leans in” to support each other by sharing stories of success and failures with the hope to inspire others. We wish to provide students with mentorship and a space for their holistic growth in tech and non-tech domains. Lean In IIIT SKLM currently has six circles: Programming for Placements, Machine Learning, Open Source, Design, Creative Writing, and Public Speaking, with over 160 mentees and 25 mentors! Along with this we also frequently organise panel sessions, invited talks and hackathons. Our circles and sessions are open to all IIIT SKLM students!

                 

Bootcamp Guidelines

  • At least 15 active members and 3 co-ordinators are required for any bootcamp to exist. The 3 co-ordinators can be a part of the 15 active members.

  • There must be atleast an activity per week.

  • The internal website of the club should be regularly updated. It shall, at least, have an updated list of bootcamp members, events list, budget details and contact details of co-ordinators.

  • If any bootcamp has a requirement of a visiting instructor, there must be at least 15 interested students who will benefit from it.

  • The tentative annual budget should be provided to the Student Council (when the new session starts). The budget can be ZERO depending on the nature of the group. The club must give a justification for the usage of money, and a strong justification must be provided if the requirement is more than Rs. 1000. The detailed breakdown of the budget (in terms of events, inventory, etc.) should be provided as well.
